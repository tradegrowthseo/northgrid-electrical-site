Why NorthGrid Was Founded

Built to serve the clients that compliance lets down

The compliance sector has a problem that most people in it won't admit to. Too many electrical contractors treat EICRs and periodic inspection as a revenue stream rather than a specialism. Reports get issued quickly, corners get cut, defects get missed or misclassified, and the documentation that lands on a housing association's desk or an FM manager's portal doesn't hold up when it's scrutinised.

FM companies end up chasing re-inspections. Housing associations carry liability they don't know they have. Property managers find themselves explaining why a report passed three months ago and now needs remedial works the contractor should have caught the first time.

"NorthGrid was founded because compliance clients deserve a contractor who actually understands compliance — the documentation, the audit trails, the reactive response protocols, and what it means for a report to pass scrutiny first time."

NorthGrid Electrical was deliberately built to serve the FM and commercial space — the organisations with planned maintenance schedules, multi-site contracts, RAMS requirements and real consequences when the paperwork isn't right. That shaped everything: the approach to reporting, the detail in certification, the willingness to work to FM timescales, and the discipline that comes from years working in regulated, high-consequence environments including aviation.

It's also why NorthGrid doesn't subcontract. Consistency of standard is everything when compliance is the product. When you work with NorthGrid, you work with Matt — or with an engineer Matt is directly responsible for.
